# DM Prep Guide

## Pre-Session Checklist
- Ensure you have all materials prepared (e.g., maps, stats, character sheets)
- Review the main plot points and any player backstories that are relevant
- Set the scene: Know where the players will begin and what their objectives are

## NPC Dialogue Starters
- "What brings you to this part of the world?"
- "I've heard rumors of danger in the area; you should tread carefully."
- "If I were you, I'd keep my eyes peeled for strange occurrences."

## Atmosphere and Description Examples
- **Atmosphere**: Dimly lit caverns filled with faint echoes of dripping water, with a musty odor lingering in the air.
- **Description**: A vibrant marketplace buzzing with the sounds of haggling merchants and lively townsfolk, colorful banners fluttering in the breeze.

## Environmental Hazard Mechanics
- **Traps**: Describe the mechanics and how players can detect or avoid them.
- **Weather Conditions**: Effects of heavy rain or snow on visibility and movement.

## Combat Tips
- Utilize terrain: Encourage players to use the environment to their advantage.
- Keep descriptions vivid: Include not just mechanics but the sensory details of the combat.

## Pacing and Tension Management
- Use cliffhangers at the end of sessions to keep players engaged.
- Control pacing by varying the rhythm of combat and roleplay encounters.

## Contingency Plans
- Prepare alternative paths if players deviate from the expected storyline.
- Include NPCs that can offer hints or redirect players if stuck.

## Treasure and Rewards
- Create a balanced reward system: Gold, items, and story developments.
- Consider unique treasures that tie into character backstories or future plots.

## Resources to Prepare
- Rulebooks and supplements that are relevant to your campaign.
- Online forums and podcasts for additional tips and insights.
- Apps or tools for managing encounters and tracking player progress.
