# Session Notes: The Rot in the Roots

> DM prep guide for all 8 sessions. Includes checklists, dialogue, atmosphere, combat tips, and pacing.

---

## Pre-Session Checklist (Universal)

Before every session, confirm you have:

- [ ] Session's key scenes and encounters outlined (see [ENCOUNTERS.md](./ENCOUNTERS.md))
- [ ] Relevant NPC motivations fresh in mind (see [CHARACTERS.md](./CHARACTERS.md))
- [ ] Location descriptions ready (see [LOCATIONS.md](./LOCATIONS.md))
- [ ] Any handout props prepared (scanner audio, scale prop, cassette label, newspaper clipping)
- [ ] Theselhydra arc position confirmed (what stage is it at this session?)
- [ ] Ambient audio/playlist ready (low synth, static bursts, bass drones -- no lyrics)
- [ ] Current party level confirmed; leveling checkpoint noted if applicable
- [ ] Session 1 only: player character hooks reviewed; opening scene memorized

**Table setup atmosphere:**
- Dim the lights or use a lamp instead of overhead lighting
- Play ambient sound from the start of session (don't announce it)
- Have a physical "clue envelope" ready -- the physical prop creates investment

## NPC Dialogue Starters

### Margaret Thorne (Librarian)
- *"I've kept this file for thirty years. I told myself it was just being thorough. I think I was waiting for someone to ask."*
- *"The animal attacks stopped being reported after 1988. Not because they stopped. Because someone asked the newspaper editor not to print them."*
- *"I'm not going into those woods with you. But I will have the 1987 survey maps ready by the time you get back."*

### Sheriff Blackwell
- *"I know what you think you saw. I need you to understand what happens to people who don't let this go."* (early)
- *"I made the wrong call twenty years ago. I'm not making it again."* (later, if trust was built)
- *"I can give you three hours before I have to make a report. Don't waste it."*

### Deputy Reyes
- *"I've been here four months and I've already learned that 'federal matter' is code for 'we don't want to know.'"*
- *"Off the record? I ran those plates. That vehicle doesn't exist according to DMV."*
- *"I'm not a hero. I'm just someone who reads the paperwork."*

### Dr. Helena Voss
- *"You're standing in a facility that has made more documented contact with another dimension than any science program in history. Show some respect for the work."* (defensive)
- *"The sealing procedure was classified impractical because no one wanted to lose the data. Not because it wouldn't work."* (if pressed)
- *"I knew about the Chen boy. I made a decision. I genuinely believed pulling him out was more dangerous than monitoring."* (if confronted)
- *"What does the data show? If the data shows I was wrong, I will act on the data. That's all I've ever done."* (pivot toward ally)

### Marcus Chen (Session 7)
- *"I counted seventeen days. I think I miscounted. I don't think it matters anymore."*
- *"The thing with the five heads -- it checks on me. It drives the vine things away. I don't know what that means but it's true."*
- *"I recorded a tape. For my parents. In case. You can take it back if -- when we go back."*
- *"I named it six months ago. I dreamed it and put it in my notes and named it Theselhydra. How did I know?"*

## Atmosphere Descriptions by Location

### Oakhaven High (normal)
*"The hallway smells like floor wax and cafeteria garlic bread. The homecoming banner has ACORNS #1 in orange and brown. Lockers bang open and shut. Everything is completely normal and somehow that's worse now."*

### The Dead Zone (woods, Session 3+)
*"The grass goes from alive to dead in a line so clean it looks drawn on the ground. No transition. Just -- here it is alive, there it is not. Your breath fogs on the wrong side of October. The compass needle spins slowly, like it's looking for something it can't find."*

### Millbrook Creek (near the gate)
*"The water runs black for about twenty yards downstream of the bend. Not murky -- black, like ink diluting at the edges. It smells like copper and something older. Your walkie fills with a low clicking sound, rhythmic, like something breathing slowly. Then it stops."*

### The Old Mill Basement
*"Poured concrete, sometime in the nineties. The screens show a map of Oakhaven with the gate site as a pulsing red dot. The dot is larger than it was in the last log entry. In the northeast corner, something has pressed vines and black material into a sleeping place for something very large. The claw marks on the wall go ten feet up. There are five distinct toes in each mark."*

### Nexus Research Facility
*"It feels like a hospital retrofitted for field work. Whiteboards everywhere, but the equations look like containment schedules. A break room with decent coffee. On the whiteboard: 'DO NOT APPROACH SOUTH ZONE PERIOD.'"*

### The Upside Down (general)
*"Cold that doesn't match the season. Ash drifts from a sky with no sun -- just a gray-purple permanent twilight. The vines glow faint blue-green from inside. The street is recognizably your street. The houses are your houses. Everything is right. Everything is wrong. Your phone shows nothing. Your compass spins."*

### The Mirror-Chen House (Session 7)
*"Marker on every wall -- Marcus's handwriting. Inventory lists, day counts, a map of this version of your town annotated: 'SAFE ZONE -- vine things avoid it.' In the corner: a flashlight on its last battery, a cassette player, and a tape labeled HOME (UNFINISHED). And Marcus Chen, sitting with his back to the wall, who looks up and says: 'I counted seventeen days. I think I miscounted.'"*

## Environmental Hazard Quick Reference

| Hazard | Trigger | Effect | Counter |
|--------|---------|--------|---------|
| Black Goo Patch | Movement through affected area | 1d4 acid per turn; Difficult Terrain | Fire; Nexus PPE |
| Spore Cloud | 4+ hours in Upside Down | CON DC 12 or 1 Exhaustion | Breathing filter; shorter visits |
| Rot Pulse | Hourly d6 in Upside Down (1 = trigger) | Creatures incoming in 3 rounds | Theselhydra territory (if trusted) |
| Vine Wall | Navigation through Upside Down | Impassable without 10 min cutting or fire | Axes, knives, torches |
| Electronic Failure | Proximity to gate / Upside Down | Phones dead; compass spins; radio static | Accept the loss; physical navigation |
| Corrupted Deer Charge | Deer moves 20+ ft toward target | 2d6 extra + STR DC 13 or Prone + goo | Avoid line-of-sight; height advantage |
| Rot Exposure (human) | Goo contact or long-term proximity | Behavioral drift; DC 13 WIS save | Remove from exposure; gate seal stops spread |

## Combat Tips by Creature

### Shadow Swarm
- Lights flicker before it arrives. Static builds.
- Bright light is the counter. UV flashlight = advantage on attacks + visible recoil.
- Session 1 swarm should not kill anyone. It retreats when light is brought.

### Vine Creatures
- Fire is their weakness -- and starting a fire in the mill or Upside Down has consequences. Play that tension.
- False Appearance trait: describe a "dead shrub" the party has to walk past.
- Larger variant's Constrict = one PC Restrained while others manage the fight. Perfect Session 4 chaos.

### The Theselhydra (Non-Combat)
- Never attack first unless the PCs push it.
- Use audio: low bass hum before it appears. Silence from all other animals.
- Five heads = five directions of attention. It already knows they're there. It's choosing to allow or not allow.
- The "Celeste says Marcus Chen" moment: pause the session. Let it breathe. All five heads turn simultaneously. Don't roll Initiative yet. Make them decide first.

### The Theselhydra (Combat -- Session 6)
- It targets Nexus contractors preferentially. Uses Coil (bludgeoning) on PCs rather than Bite.
- If PCs back down visibly (weapons stowed, one full round no attacks), it pauses and assesses. The fight can end here.
- The trust outcome of this scene is the emotional fulcrum of the campaign. If the PCs earn it, Session 8 lands.

### The Demogorgon (Session 8)
- Do not stat-fight this. Cinematic sequence.
- Presence turns: describe what it does when not engaged (circling, scenting, moving closer, killing something nearby).
- If the Theselhydra engages it: set piece, not dice. They watch. They complete the seal. They don't know which one survived until later.

## Pacing and Tension Management

**Sessions 1-2 (Mystery):** Slow, grounded. Every scene could be normal small-town drama -- one detail is wrong. Reward investigation.

**Session 3 (First Escalation):** The bike chase is the turning point. End on the Theselhydra first contact. Do not continue past it. Let it sit overnight.

**Sessions 4-5 (Investigation/Conspiracy):** Vary the pace. Old Mill = horror dungeon crawl. Nexus Research = thriller heist. The contrast is intentional and good.

**Session 6 (Escalation):** Feel big. Town-wide consequences. The Theselhydra confrontation should be the longest scene of the campaign so far. The Upside Down choice made under time pressure.

**Session 7 (Survival):** Slow, tense. Roll the Rot Pulse die audibly. Navigation challenges feel real. Finding Marcus = emotional high point of the campaign.

**Session 8 (Finale):** Start fast, stay fast. Sealing challenge is a countdown. Demogorgon arrives Round 3. Theselhydra choice = last major PC decision. Seal completes or it doesn't. Epilogue: slow, quiet, earned.

**Cliffhanger Placement:**
- Session 1: something tall at the tree line
- Session 2: Nexus SUV slow drive-by, plates that don't exist
- Session 3: the Theselhydra, clear, visible, five heads
- Session 4: bent cage bars, the gate expanding on the monitoring screen
- Session 5: Marcus listed as "MONITORING" -- they know, they're doing nothing
- Session 6: the gate is open, choice point
- Session 7: Marcus home, the cassette, the handwriting on the wall
- Session 8: epilogue, the final flicker

## Contingency Plans

**Players miss a key clue:** Margaret Thorne has backup copies of almost everything important in her binder.

**Players go to police in Session 1:** Blackwell files minimum report, sends them home. Reyes slips them her number on the way out: *"If you find something real, call me first."*

**Players try to ally with Nexus early:** Voss meets with them, denies everything, has contractors follow them for 48 hours. Makes Session 5 more charged.

**Players skip Nexus infiltration (Session 5):** Sealing procedure is in the Old Mill monitoring equipment offline cache. DC 16 to find without context from Session 5.

**Players avoid Theselhydra in Session 6:** They can reach the gate, but it will be between them and the gate on return. The confrontation is unavoidable; the outcome is a player choice.

**PC compromised by Rot:** Early stage = behavioral drift + DC 13 WIS save to resist. Roleplaying opportunity, not a death sentence. Sealing the gate stops progression. See [LORE.md](./LORE.md) for full guidance.

## Treasure and Rewards

No gold. What players collect: **information, access, allies, and gear.**

| Session | Reward Type | Examples |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Information | Scanner recording, goo sample, Marcus's D&D notes |
| 2 | Information + Ally | Newspaper clippings; Deputy Reyes's number |
| 3 | Evidence | Nexus contractor badge, dead zone map |
| 4 | Access + Evidence | Nexus keycard, incident report fragment |
| 5 | Critical Info | Sealing procedure files, Voss's project maps, Marcus status |
| 6 | Narrative | Theselhydra trust earned (or not) |
| 7 | Marcus + Information | His mirror-map, the cassette, the handwriting on the wall |
| 8 | Resolution | Sealed gate, Marcus home, Oakhaven changed but surviving |

**Modern gear rewards:**
- UV flashlight (Session 1, hardware store)
- Nexus hazmat breathing filter (Session 4-5)
- Nexus keycard (Session 4)
- Emergency signal flare (Session 5)
- LED string with frequency tuner (Session 7 prep, for Upside Down communication)

**No magic items.** If a player asks: *"Your gear is your knowledge. Your allies are your power. Your plan is your weapon."*
