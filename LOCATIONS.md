# Locations: The Rot in the Roots

> All locations are in **Oakhaven, Ohio, 2026**. Their Upside Down mirror versions are noted where relevant.

---

## Oakhaven Geography Overview

Oakhaven sits in a shallow valley with Millbrook Creek running southwest to northeast across the southern half of town. The creek is not scenic — it's a working drainage system, wide and slow, with a concrete channel near downtown that opens into a natural bed as it approaches the woods. The woods are oak and ash, roughly 600 acres, bounded by farm fields and the county highway.

The **dead zone** — the area where the Rot's influence is strongest — begins about 200 yards south of the high school stadium and extends into the woods toward the creek. Animals avoid it. Leaves rot faster there. Snow, in winter, melts in concentric rings outward from its center like something below is generating heat.

## Key Locations

---

### 1. Oakhaven High School
**Type:** School (large, 1970s construction, recent additions)
**Sessions:** 1, 2, 3, 5 (background), 6

**Description:**
A brick-and-glass block of educational architecture that smells permanently of industrial cleaner and cafeteria gravy. Three floors, a basement, a gymnasium, a football stadium out back (the Oakhaven Acorns — there was a vote about the mascot, and the Acorns won). Homecoming banners are up in October.

**Key Areas:**
- **D&D Club Room (Room 114):** Where the PCs meet, plan, and name the things they're facing. A chalkboard with creature diagrams that gets increasingly accurate as the campaign progresses.
- **Library/Media Center:** Ms. Hargrove's domain. Computers with filters. A printer that always jams.
- **Coach Hendricks' Field House Storage Room:** Padlocked. Smells wrong. Drips occasionally from a crack in the floor.
- **The Back Field / Stadium Perimeter:** Where the dead zone begins. Grass goes brown here first. The bleachers on the south end are rarely used. The scoreboard light on the south end flickers in Rot-proximity.
- **Basement Hallway (B-wing):** A teacher has been "renovating" a storage area here. New locks, new smell.

**Upside Down Mirror:** Oakhaven High is intact in structure but overgrown. Every classroom holds furniture but is draped in dark vines. The cafeteria is full of standing water. The gymnasium's roof has partially collapsed. The D&D club room is the one space where the vines thin out — as if something has been keeping it clear. There is a cassette tape on the table: *Marcus's handwriting on the label.*

### 2. Downtown Oakhaven
**Type:** Small-town commercial district
**Sessions:** 1, 2, 4 (peripherally), 6 (lockdown)

**Description:**
Four blocks of storefronts, two of which are occupied. The dollar-burger diner (Patty's), a hardware store (Brennan's), the used bookshop, a bar (The Watershed, neon half-broken), a dollar store, a pharmacy, and the county library.

**Key Areas:**
- **Patty's Diner:** The town's information exchange. Fyndor picks up scanner traffic here. A booth in the back has seen more plans than any war room.
- **Oakhaven Public Library:** Margaret Thorne's kingdom. Houses the 1987 newspaper archive in physical binders. One drawer of the card catalog is labeled "Environmental Incidents" and has been there so long nobody remembers adding it.
- **The Watershed Bar:** One of the Nexus Research field contractors drinks here. They talk when they drink.
- **Brennan's Hardware:** Sells UV lights, batteries, zip ties, duct tape, wire, bolt cutters, and (if asked the right way) propane torches. The owner doesn't ask questions about large purchases.

**Upside Down Mirror:** Downtown Oakhaven exists in the Upside Down but is barely recognizable. Buildings have merged with organic growth. Patty's Diner roof has caved in and something has nested in the kitchen. The library is the most structurally intact building, for reasons the PCs may speculate on.

### 3. The Chen House
**Type:** Residential
**Sessions:** 1, 2, 7 (communication anchor)

**Description:**
A two-story colonial on Birch Lane, about four blocks from the high school. Normal house. Family photos. Homework on the kitchen table. Marcus's room is on the second floor — his D&D notes are still spread on his desk.

**Key Items in Marcus's Room:**
- Campaign notes labeled "Theselhydra — five-headed water guardian, serpentine, not malevolent"
- A string of Christmas lights plugged into the wall (how the lights-communication method begins)
- A partial map of Oakhaven's creek system, annotated with water temperatures
- A cassette labeled "HOME (unfinished)"

**Upside Down Mirror:** The Chen house exists and is one of the less-decayed structures in the Upside Down's version of the neighborhood. Marcus is using it as a base. He has cleared the vines from one room and written notes on the walls in permanent marker (inventory of what he has, days he thinks have passed, messages to whoever might come through).

### 4. Millbrook Creek
**Type:** Waterway / Dead Zone
**Sessions:** 2 (omen), 3 (first contact), 6 (gate opens), 7 (entry point)

**Description:**
South of the high school, Millbrook Creek widens and slows as it enters the old growth section of the woods. The banks here are soft and smell of rot-fallen oak. There is a concrete culvert where the creek goes under the old county road, and downstream of it the water runs black for about 20 yards in every season.

The **gate** is under the water, in a depression in the creek bed, about 3 feet below the surface at its deepest. In dry months the depression is sometimes exposed. The water around it is always cold, always slightly viscous.

**Signs of Rot Proximity:**
- Compass deviation within 40 yards
- Animals avoiding the east bank entirely
- A low, subsonic hum audible on certain scanner frequencies
- The black viscosity in the water (the goo concentrates here)
- A ring of dead earth, perfect circle, about 15-foot radius, in the bank grass

**The Gate:**
It looks like a wound in the world — not a glowing portal, but a tear. Edges of black membrane, organic, pulsing slowly. The smell is overwhelming: copper, autumn leaves, ozone. It is 6 feet across in Session 6. When fully open it is 10 feet across and barely containable.

**Theselhydra Connection:** The Theselhydra's territory in the Upside Down encompasses this crossing point. When it is not actively moving through, its tracks (five-toed, heavy) circle the gate site in the Upside Down within a 200-yard radius. In Session 2, the PCs can find a single scale — iridescent black, about the size of a dinner plate — on the bank of the creek near the dead zone edge.

### 5. The Old Mill
**Type:** Abandoned industrial building with concealed underground facility
**Sessions:** 4 (dungeon crawl), 5 (referenced), 6 (combat zone), 7 (entry method)

**Description:**
A three-story grain processing mill built in 1932, decommissioned in 1989. Boarded windows, warning signs, chain-link perimeter that was recently repaired (you can see where new links were woven in — different color metal). Inside: dust, rusted machinery, grain conveyors, and — if you know what to look for — fresh cable runs along the north wall, numbered biohazard tags, and a trapdoor under the main floor that leads down.

**The Basement Level:**
Not original to the building. Poured concrete, 1990s construction. Currently used by Nexus Research as a secondary access point and equipment cache for the gate site. Contains:
- Monitoring equipment (screens showing gate status, Rot spread measurements)
- Emergency containment gear (hazmat suits, CO₂ fire suppression, biohazard bags)
- A caged area that used to hold something (large cage, bent bars, fresh scoring in the concrete)
- A keycard reader on a locked door

**Loot/Evidence:**
- **Nexus Research keycard** (low clearance, gets through the front door of the facility)
- **Incident report fragment:** "...expansion of aperture beyond 4.2m risk threshold... VOSS-7 protocol suspended pending... containment asset behavior... classified..."
- **A handwritten note on a legal pad:** "It's not staying in the south quadrant. It moves toward human contact. Recommend re-sedation schedule."

**Upside Down Mirror:** The Old Mill in the Upside Down is structurally intact but vines have completely consumed the upper two floors. The basement level is partially flooded and serves as a Vine Creature nesting area (5–8 present). A massive nest-mound of compressed vine and black goo sits in the northeast corner — this is the edge of the Theselhydra's territory.

### 6. Nexus Research (Oakhaven Site)
**Type:** Government-adjacent private facility
**Sessions:** 5 (infiltration), 6 (confrontation), 8 (possible finale location)

**Description:**
Located on the eastern edge of the industrial park, a cluster of low, flat buildings behind an 8-foot chain-link fence topped with razor wire. The sign says "Nexus Environmental Solutions — Wetland Restoration Division." The parking lot has eight vehicles: four SUVs, two vans, and two trucks with equipment in the beds.

Inside: the building is organized around a central server room and a main lab space. It feels like a cross between a tech startup and a field hospital — whiteboards covered in equations, equipment cases on dollies, a break room with good coffee and a coffee-stained briefing schedule. Dr. Voss's office is at the back.

**Security:**
- Two guards at front gate (day), one (night)
- Card access on all internal doors
- Camera coverage of exterior and hallways
- One motion-sensor zone (server room) that requires a DC 16 Stealth check or an Electronics toolkit check (DC 14) to bypass
- A "response contractor" team of four can be on-site in 8 minutes if alerted

**Key Areas:**
- **Main Lab:** Biological samples from the Upside Down. The black goo in glass vials. A growth culture that is definitely still growing.
- **Dr. Voss's Office:** Personnel files, project timeline, a wall-sized map of Oakhaven with the gate site, the dead zone radius, the Old Mill, and (circled in red) the dead zone boundary that expanded in September.
- **Server Room:** Contains years of gate monitoring data, communications with federal oversight, and a critical file: a full sealing procedure developed in 1992 and classified as "impractical." It is the ritual the PCs will use in Session 8.
- **Detention Cell (unofficial):** A room that looks like an office but has a bolt on the outside of the door. Used for "civilian exposure management." Currently empty.

### 7. The Upside Down (Mirror-Oakhaven)
**Type:** Parallel dimension
**Sessions:** 7 (primary), 8 (partially)

**Description:**
The Upside Down is Oakhaven, reflected — same streets, same buildings, same geography — but drained of light and warmth, and consumed by the Rot. It exists in a permanent twilight of ash-colored sky and bioluminescent vine growth that pulses faintly with a sickly blue-green light.

**Sensory Details:**
- Air: breathable but thick. Tastes like copper and dead leaves. Spores drift constantly, catching light like snow.
- Sound: everything echoes slightly wrong. Footsteps sound like they come from the wrong direction.
- Smell: decay, wet earth, old iron.
- Sight: no sunlight. The light comes from the vines. Shadows don't fall in sensible directions.
- Temperature: 15–20 degrees colder than the real Oakhaven, always.

**Navigation:**
The streets correspond to the real Oakhaven streets, but distances vary. Some streets are blocked by vine walls (impassable without tools or fire). Others have been partially cleared by something large — the Theselhydra's movement through the area has left corridors through the growth.

**The Theselhydra's Territory:**
Center at Millbrook Creek. 400-yard radius of "safe" passage (Rot Pulse rolls at disadvantage, Vine Creatures avoid the area). Marked by claw gouges in surfaces, shed scales, and a characteristic sound: low resonant vibration, like a large animal breathing.

**Marcus's Location:**
The mirror-version of his bedroom, corner of Birch Lane and Oak Street. Walls covered in notes. A single functioning flashlight on its last battery. A series of marks on the wall tracking time. He is alive, thin, dirty, and desperately glad to see another human face.
