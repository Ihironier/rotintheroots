## Shadow Swarm

**Flavor:** The Rot's smallest agents — fragments of darkness that move like starlings and hunger like something much larger. The PCs call them "shadow motes" or just "the swarm."

**Description:** A roiling mass of shadow, each piece roughly hand-sized, that flows and clusters like murmurating birds. They don't fully solidify. Electronics flicker in their presence. They are drawn to warmth.

## Black Goo Hazard

**Flavor:** The physical substance of the Rot. Not a creature — a hazard. It appears at gate sites, on compromised animals, and as a residue after combat with Rot-touched creatures.

**Properties:**
- Corrosive to certain metals (aluminum dissolves in 1 hour of contact; iron takes 4 hours). Does not affect steel, wood, or plastic.
- Glows faintly under UV light (blue-white, slow crawling movement pattern).
- Living: has slow autonomous movement (1 inch per minute), oriented toward organic warmth.
- Psychically conductive: creatures who touch it bare-handed sometimes hear a low, rhythmic sound (the pulse of the Rot). DC 13 WIS save or be Unsettled (disadvantage on Insight and Perception checks) for 1 hour.

**Goo Patch (as environmental hazard):**
- A 5-foot square of goo on the ground is Difficult Terrain.
- Any creature that starts its turn in a goo patch takes 2 (1d4) acid damage.
- Fire destroys a goo patch in one round (creates a foul black smoke — CON save DC 12 or disadvantaged on CON checks for 1 minute).

## The Theselhydra

**Flavor:** Named by Marcus Chen six months ago from his D&D campaign notes: a five-headed water serpent, not malevolent, but territorial and immense. What he named, correctly, from recurring notes in his creature journal: a massive apex predator of the Upside Down, native to dark water environments, with five independent heads on a single body that can act semi-autonomously.

The Theselhydra is not the Rot. It is a creature of the Upside Down that the Rot has not yet consumed — partly because of its size and vitality, partly because it has been (until recently) tolerated as a useful predator by whatever intelligence the Rot serves. It has been held in a state of partial compliance by Nexus Research using a chemical/sonic regimen. As that regimen fails, it is increasingly itself.

**DM Note:** The Theselhydra is a **recurring character**, not just a stat block. See [ENCOUNTERS.md](./ENCOUNTERS.md) for its arc across Sessions 2–8. Only use combat stats when the PCs force a direct engagement. Otherwise, the Theselhydra is an encounter of presence, communication, and decision.

## Rot Source

**Flavor:** Not a creature. A locus — a point in the Upside Down where the Rot concentrates, pulses, and radiates outward. It looks like a tumor made of black goo and vine, roughly 10 feet across, pulsing with slow bioluminescent light.

**As a Hazard/Encounter Objective:**
- Creatures within 30 feet of a Rot Source at the start of their turn make a DC 13 WIS save or take 5 (2d4) psychic damage and have disadvantage on their next attack roll.
- Vine Creatures are immune to this effect and are drawn to Rot Sources (1d4+1 spawn per hour within 100 feet of an unsecured Rot Source).
- Destroying a Rot Source requires dealing 40 HP of fire damage to it, completing a sealing ritual (10-minute skill challenge, DC 12 per stage), or using the Nexus sealing procedure (see Session 8).

## Demogorgon (Cinematic Apex Predator)

**Flavor:** The PCs name this creature from their D&D vocabulary — and it fits. An apex predator of the Upside Down, massive, flower-faced, moving on four limbs with terrifying speed. It is drawn to the gate in Session 8 by the Rot surge.

**DM Note:** The Demogorgon is not meant to be fought directly at level 4. It is a cinematic pressure — a threat that forces movement, decision, and sacrifice. Its presence in Session 8 is the equivalent of the T-rex in Jurassic Park: awe, terror, run.
