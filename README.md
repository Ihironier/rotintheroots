# 🌑 The Rot in the Roots

> *"Something came back with the dog. Something that didn't belong."*

A modern-day horror campaign for **D&D 5e**, set in **Oakhaven, Ohio — 2026**.

---

## Premise

Marcus Chen has been missing for three days. His dog came back alone — shaking, matted with a black, acrid goo that eats through cheap metal. The police say he probably ran off. The news truck left by Tuesday morning. But the lights on Elm Street keep flickering in patterns, and something tall is watching from the tree line behind the school stadium.

Oakhaven is a small town with a rotting secret underneath it — and a tear in reality that someone has been keeping open on purpose.

**The Rot in the Roots** is an **8-session D&D 5e campaign** inspired by the aesthetics and tone of *Stranger Things*: bikes and flashlights, government cover-ups, shadow-choked mirror worlds, and kids who refuse to look away. No lords. No dragons. Just Oakhaven, Ohio, and whatever is coming through the gate.

## The Theselhydra

The Theselhydra is not just a monster — it is the campaign's beating heart. First glimpsed in Session 2 as a river omen, it becomes a recurring presence across all 8 sessions: territorial guardian, Nexus Research "asset," reluctant protector of Marcus Chen, and ultimately an ally or obstacle in the final seal. What the players decide to do with the Theselhydra will define the tone of their ending.

## Stranger Things DNA

This campaign is built on five pillars:

- **Kids with gear, not heroes with treasure** — bikes, flashlights, walkies, a police scanner, bolt cutters
- **Analog weirdness** — cassette tapes, CRT static, radios detuning, Christmas lights as communication
- **Body-snatcher paranoia** — a trusted adult isn't acting right, and nobody can prove it yet
- **Government cover-up** — Nexus Research is the public face; containment is the private mission
- **The Upside Down** — ash, spores, predatory vines, glitching electronics, time feels wrong
