# Lore: The Rot in the Roots

> Deep world-building for **Oakhaven, Ohio, 2026**. Contains spoilers. DM eyes only (or share selectively as the campaign progresses).

---

## The Upside Down: Origins

The Upside Down is not another planet. It is not a hell dimension. It is a **parallel state of the same physical space** — Oakhaven, Ohio, overlaid on itself like a photographic double-exposure, out of phase by a dimension that has no agreed-upon name in physics or folklore.

It is not empty. It has its own ecology, its own weather (of a kind), its own hierarchy of life. Its sky is a permanent ashen twilight. Its plant life is all derived from the same root organism — the Rot's physical substrate — which spreads like a mycelial network under everything and surfaces as the vine walls, the tendril growths, the nest-mounds.

It has been there as long as Oakhaven has been Oakhaven. Possibly longer. The boundary between the two states is thin in certain places, and has been since before the town was built. The creek bed over the mineral formation at the south end of town is the thinnest point. Under the right conditions — specific electromagnetic states, possibly emotional resonance, almost certainly some geological factor no one has fully characterized — the barrier yields.

**What is NOT true:** There is no ancient evil that created the Upside Down. There is no god, demon, or cosmic force with intentions. The Rot is not malevolent in the way a person is malevolent. It spreads because spreading is what it does. It consumes because consuming is what it does. It is a force of nature that happens to be lethal.

This is scarier than a villain.

## The Nature of the Rot

The Rot is the Upside Down's dominant organism — a planet-spanning (or at least continent-spanning) mycelial network that permeates every surface of that dimension. It is the Upside Down's equivalent of soil bacteria, decomposer fungi, and nervous system simultaneously.

In its home dimension, the Rot is in equilibrium. It grows, it breaks down, it maintains. The Vine Creatures are extensions of it — mobile fruiting bodies, essentially. The black goo is its metabolic byproduct, secreted at the boundaries of its growth zones.

**What happens at a gate:** When the boundary tears, the Rot begins to extend into our dimension. It does so slowly at first — the goo leaking through, affecting nearby organisms, weakening the boundary further. Animals are affected first (smaller nervous systems, closer to the ground where the goo pools). Humans take longer but are not immune.

**What the Rot does to people:** Extended exposure without protective gear causes:
- Behavioral drift: reduction in complex social reasoning, increase in territorial instinct
- Physical signs: black veining under the skin (visible to UV), temperature sensitivity, decreased appetite
- Advanced stage: near-total compromise (the "body snatcher" state) — the person is still present but the Rot's network has threaded through their nervous system and has partial control of behavior

Coach Hendricks is at early-to-middle stage. He can be helped if the breach near the field house is sealed. He is not a villain. He is a victim.

**What the Rot is NOT doing:** It is not trying to invade our world. It has no plan. It is not the Mind Flayer with intentions. The "intelligence" the Rot seems to have is the same intelligence a mycelial network has — it responds to stimuli, it grows toward resources, it maintains equilibrium. At scale, this looks like intentionality. It is not.

## The Government's Role: Nexus Research

**Full name:** Nexus Environmental Solutions, LLC (federal contractor designation: NX-SHADOW-7)

**History:**
- 1987: The Oakhaven gate first opens during a severe drought. Three people vanish. A federal team arrives within 72 hours — someone was already watching this location.
- 1988: Nexus Research formally established as a contractor to manage the gate. Mission (original): contain, study, close. The "close" portion was quietly deprioritized within 18 months.
- 1990–2019: Gate maintained in stable configuration. Annual incident reports filed with a federal oversight committee that has six members and no public record. The community absorbed the cover stories.
- 2019: Dr. Helena Voss assigned to the Oakhaven site as lead researcher.
- 2022: Voss proposes "aperture expansion" to increase sample throughput. Approved by oversight committee (3-2 vote).
- 2024: Expansion begins. The gate goes from 2 feet across to 4.2 feet across. The dead zone radius triples.
- September 2026: Voss's expansion attempt to reach 6 feet triggers a Rot surge. The Theselhydra's compliance breaks down. The dead zone expands rapidly.

**What Nexus Knows That the PCs Don't (Until Session 5):**
- The gate has been open for 39 years
- They have been extracting biological samples from the Upside Down for three decades
- They have extensive knowledge of Rot biology, creature behavior (including the Theselhydra), and gate mechanics
- The sealing procedure exists. It was developed in 1992. They chose not to use it because closing the gate would end the research program.
- Marcus Chen is "Civilian Exposure Event 2026-10-07." He is listed as alive on their monitoring feeds (they have sensors in the Upside Down). They have made no rescue attempt.

## The 1980s Connection

The three people who vanished in 1987:
1. **Thomas Whitfield** (32) — local farmer. Vanished near the creek during the drought while checking water levels. Never found.
2. **Angela Moreau** (52) — librarian. Celeste's grandmother. Vanished during what was reported as a hiking accident. The "accident" report was filed by a federal agent.
3. **Dr. Paul Nguyen** (28) — a visiting hydrologist from a university, doing drought research. Vanished the same night as Thomas Whitfield. His field notes were confiscated.

**What actually happened:** The gate opened. All three were drawn to the tear — either by proximity, curiosity, or (in Angela Moreau's case) possibly something else. They fell through. The federal team that arrived found no bodies. The gate was partially sealed (reduced to a manageable 2-foot aperture) but not closed.

**Angela Moreau in the Upside Down:** There is one more thing in the Upside Down's mirror-Chen house. It is faint, partial, and the DM can decide whether it is a ghost, a psychic impression, a Rot-maintained echo, or simply a trick of the light. But Celeste, if she is present when Marcus is found, will recognize the handwriting on one of the walls. It is not Marcus's. It is much older. It is her grandmother's, and it says: *"I found the water. Don't follow the sound."*

Whether Angela Moreau is alive in there, dead, or something in between is left to the DM and the campaign's needs. This thread can remain unresolved — not every mystery needs an answer.

## Gate Mechanics

**Formation:** Gates form at points of thin dimensional boundary — geological, electromagnetic, or sometimes (theoretically) emotional. The Oakhaven gate is geological (the mineral formation under the creek bed acts as a conductor for whatever force drives the boundary thinning).

**Stability:** A gate naturally tends toward closure — the two dimensions repel each other like mismatched magnets. Nexus Research has been applying a stabilization field (electromagnetic, specific frequency) to keep the gate open. This is why the gate has been stable for 39 years. Without the stabilizer, the gate would close in approximately 72 hours.

**Expansion:** Forcing the gate wider — as Voss attempted — requires overcoming the natural repulsion. It is energetically expensive and destabilizing. The 2026 surge is the result of the gate boundary being strained beyond its stable point.

**Closing the Gate:**
- **Natural closure:** Remove the Nexus stabilizer. Gate closes in 72 hours. The Rot breach in Oakhaven will take weeks to months to clear, but the active threat ends.
- **The Sealing Ritual (1992 procedure):** Actively collapses the gate by resonating the boundary from both sides simultaneously. Faster (minutes, not hours) but requires someone on both sides. Works while the Demogorgon is present.
- **Physical collapse:** Destroying the physical structure of the gate site (the Old Mill tunnel, the creek bed formation) prevents reopening indefinitely but does not affect the existing Rot breach. Takes days to clear.

**Gate Communication:** The gate conducts certain electromagnetic frequencies. This is why the Christmas-lights alphabet method works — the specific LED frequency resonates through the boundary in a way that analog radio does not. Marcus discovered this accidentally on his third day in the Upside Down when Celeste's window lights (in the real Oakhaven) started flickering in response to his knocks on his side of the boundary.

## Creature Hierarchy

The Upside Down's ecology has three layers:

**The Rot (substrate):** The mycelial network. Passive, pervasive, not an actor in any intentional sense. Origin of all other life in the Upside Down.

**The Vine Creatures and Shadow Swarms (extensions):** Mobile expressions of the Rot network. They respond to the network's stimuli — hunger, territorial pressure, proximity to gates. They have no individual identity. They are the Rot's fingers.

**The Theselhydra (apex predator):** Independent. It evolved in the Upside Down alongside the Rot but is not part of it. It eats Vine Creatures (and presumably other things). It has kept a territory free of Rot encroachment for as long as Nexus has been monitoring the site. Whether it does this because it needs the space or because something in it actively resists the Rot is unknown.

**The Demogorgon (apex hunter, transient):** Also independent of the Rot in the sense of not being the Rot. But the Demogorgon is attracted to gates — to the energy discharge, the resource concentration, the prey that gathers near crossings. It is not permanently in the Oakhaven Upside Down region. It arrives in Session 8 because the Rot surge is a massive energy signal. It will be gone when the signal stops (one way or another).

**What the Demogorgon is NOT:** Not the final boss. Not controllable. Not intelligent in a negotiable way. Not serving the Rot. It is a shark drawn by blood in the water.

## Character Secrets

### Fyndor Kasparek
His father, David Kasparek, worked for Nexus Research in 2018 as a "water quality contractor." In Nexus's files (accessible in Session 5 with a good search) there is a personnel record: David Kasparek, Environmental Monitoring Technician, 2016–2018. Reason for departure: *"Voluntary separation — personal concerns."* A handwritten note in the margin of his exit interview summary: *"Expressed concerns about civilian risk exposure. Counseled re: NDA. Departed amicably."*

David Kasparek knew. He couldn't stay. He couldn't tell anyone. He left.

Fyndor has the right to be furious. He also has the right to understand why his father made the choice he did. This arc is the DM's to handle.

### Celeste Moreau
Her grandmother Angela is one of the 1987 missing persons. The handwriting in the mirror-Chen house is Angela's. What this means — ghost, impression, or genuine survival in some form — is left open. Celeste will need time to process this. The campaign does not need to answer it. Some things the Upside Down takes and does not fully return.

### Marcus Chen
He named the Theselhydra from his D&D notes six months before it became real. He had a recurring dream about a five-headed creature in the creek. He wrote it into his campaign as a neutral entity — a guardian, not a monster. He was, somehow, right.

Whether this is coincidence, psychic sensitivity from proximity to the gate, or something the Theselhydra itself projected across the boundary is for the DM to decide. The players will wonder. That's enough.

## Possible Endings

### Ending 1: Sealed and Scarred
The gate is closed. Marcus is home. The dead zones clear over weeks. Nexus Research is quietly investigated and dissolved (the PCs may or may not see this). Oakhaven gets a cover story and mostly believes it. One or more PCs has a scar — physical, psychic, narrative. The lights never quite flicker the same way again.

**Tone:** Hopeful but permanently changed. This is the best ending.

### Ending 2: The Sacrifice
The gate is closed, but the Theselhydra — as an ally — is sealed inside the Upside Down when the gate closes. It chose this, or it had no choice, or the distinction doesn't matter. A single scale on the creek bank is all that remains. Celeste keeps it.

**Tone:** Bittersweet. The creature that Marcus named, that kept him alive, that the party came to understand — it's gone. But what it did mattered.

### Ending 3: Voss Redeems Herself
Dr. Voss activates the Nexus emergency protocol — a full gate collapse, destroying her life's work. She does it because the data is clear: the Rot is not a resource, it is a catastrophe, and she caused it. She may or may not survive the collapse (DM's choice based on how the players have engaged with her).

**Tone:** The villain becomes human. Appropriate if the players have invested in her character arc.

### Ending 4: The Gate Stays Open (Failure State)
The ritual fails, or the PCs run, or the Demogorgon drives them out before it's complete. The gate is still open. The Rot breach in Oakhaven continues to spread. Marcus is home but Oakhaven is not safe. This is a cliffhanger, not a true ending — use it only if you are running a sequel.

**Tone:** Haunting. The lights in town never stop flickering. The porch light spells: *SOON.*

## Mysteries That May Never Be Solved

- **What is Angela Moreau?** Is the handwriting a ghost, a psychic echo, a Rot-maintained memory, or something alive? The Upside Down doesn't answer every question.
- **Why did the Theselhydra protect Marcus?** The best theories (territorial instinct, the creature's resistance to the Rot extended to him, it recognized the D&D name Marcus gave it, it simply does not want to be alone) are all valid. None are confirmed.
- **What did David Kasparek know?** The file says "personal concerns." The full story of why he left and what he saw is between Fyndor and his father, somewhere outside the campaign.
- **Is the Oakhaven gate the only one?** The 1987 federal response team arrived in 72 hours. That is very fast. Someone was already watching this location.
- **What is VOSS-7?** The scanner code Fyndor picked up in Session 1. It's Dr. Voss's project identifier. But why was it being broadcast on an open frequency? Either it was an error, or someone inside Nexus wanted it to be heard.
