# Campaign Overview: The Rot in the Roots

> *Oakhaven, Ohio. Population 12,400. Three days since Marcus Chen went missing. The dog came back alone.*

---

## Setting

**Oakhaven, Ohio — October 2026**

Oakhaven is the kind of town that shows up in a montage: grain elevators at the edge of the highway, a dollar-burger diner called Patty's that's been there since 1971, a high school football team that made regionals twice in the last decade. The downtown has a hardware store, a used bookshop, a bar with a broken neon sign, and a branch of the county library that Margaret Thorne has run for thirty-one years.

It is unremarkable in every way except one: beneath Millbrook Creek, beneath the old grain processing mill on the south end of town, there is a **tear in reality**. It has been there since 1987. Someone found it, catalogued it, and has been keeping it open.

Fall is settling in. Homecoming week banners hang over Main Street. The woods behind Oakhaven High go orange and rust. The creek smells like something old.

---

## Premise

**Marcus Chen** (17, quiet, chess club, took his dog for an evening walk on October 7th) is missing. The police filed the minimum paperwork. His parents are making calls. His dog came back at 2 AM — shaking, matted in a thick black substance that bleaches fabric and eats through aluminum foil.

The player characters are connected to Marcus — by friendship, family, proximity, or something harder to name. They are the ones who will not let it go.

What they will discover: Marcus followed his dog into the woods south of the high school stadium. The dog had found something in the creek bed — a trembling pocket of cold air, a tear in the fabric of the world, and whatever comes through it. Marcus fell in.

He is not dead. He is in the **Upside Down** — a mirror-dimension of Oakhaven: dark, cold, choked with ash and living vines. He is surviving. Barely. And something in there has been keeping him alive for reasons that will matter in the finale.

## Themes

- **Adults are not always right and institutions are not always safe.** The police, the school administration, and the county government are all failing Marcus for different reasons — willful ignorance, active suppression, or quiet fear.
- **Naming the thing gives you power over it.** The PCs give creature names from their D&D campaigns to the horrors they encounter. This is how they process the impossible.
- **The monster might not be the real enemy.** The Theselhydra is a living creature. Nexus Research is the organization that opened the gate. Which one is actually responsible for Marcus being in danger?
- **Survival costs something.** Crossing into the Upside Down has physical and mental consequences. Sealing the gate will require a sacrifice the players have to choose.
- **Small towns swallow secrets.** Oakhaven has been doing this since 1987. The community has absorbed the wrongness. That doesn't mean it's okay.

## Timeline

### Before the Campaign
- **1987:** A tear appears beneath Millbrook Creek during a drought, when the water table dropped below a mineral formation. Three townspeople vanish. The federal government quietly intervenes through a contractor called **Nexus Research**. The gate is contained — but not closed.
- **1988–2025:** Nexus Research maintains a low-profile presence in Oakhaven under the guise of an environmental monitoring company. The gate is kept "stable" (i.e., open but controlled).
- **2024:** Dr. Helena Voss takes over the Oakhaven site. She believes the Upside Down has extractable resources — biological, energetic — and begins expanding the gate's aperture for sampling.
- **September 2026:** Voss's expansion ruptures the Rot's influence radius. Dead zones appear. Animals go missing. The Theselhydra, a massive apex predator native to the Upside Down, begins pushing through.

### During the Campaign
- **October 7:** Marcus Chen's dog discovers a Rot breach near the creek. Marcus follows. He falls through.
- **October 8–14 (Sessions 1–4):** The PCs investigate, encounter the Rot, find the Old Mill.
- **October 15–16 (Session 5):** Nexus Research infiltration.
- **October 17 (Session 6):** The gate widens. The Theselhydra escalates.
- **October 18 (Session 7):** The PCs cross into the Upside Down.
- **October 18–19 (Session 8):** Finale and seal.

## Factions

### The PCs
Kids and young adults who refuse to look away. Their gear is practical (bikes, walkies, bolt cutters) and their methods are improvised. They are the only people actually trying to find Marcus.

### Nexus Research
**Goal:** Extract resources from the Upside Down. Keep the gate open, keep it secret.
**Public face:** Environmental monitoring, wetland restoration, water quality studies.
**Private operation:** Gate stabilization, creature containment, Rot research.
**Key figure:** Dr. Helena Voss (see [CHARACTERS.md](./CHARACTERS.md))
**Assets:** Security contractors, the Theselhydra (formerly), local official contacts.
**Weakness:** They do not fully understand the Rot. Their containment is failing.

### The Oakhaven Sheriff's Department
**Goal:** Keep the town calm and their federal grant money flowing.
**Sheriff Dale Blackwell** knows there is something wrong in the woods. He has been told to stay out. He chooses, most of the time, to listen. He is not evil. He is afraid.
**Deputy Reyes** is new to town, hasn't been compromised, and is the best ally the PCs can find in law enforcement.

### The Rot
Not a faction with goals. A force. An infection. It spreads. It hungers. It pulls toward consciousness. The Demogorgon that appears in Session 8 is not the Rot — it is a creature serving it, as animals serve instinct.

### The Theselhydra
A five-headed apex predator from the Upside Down. Nexus Research has been keeping it fed and semi-cooperative as a living perimeter defense. As the Rot destabilizes, the Theselhydra's bond with Nexus frays. By Session 6 it is in open conflict with the facility — and by Session 7, the PCs discover it may have been doing something stranger: keeping Marcus Chen alive in its lair, for reasons that may be territorial, maternal, or something no one has a word for.

## The Upside Down (Campaign-Specific Rules)

- **Electronics:** Phones lose signal immediately. Compasses spin randomly. Car engines stall. Radios emit wet static with occasional voices.
- **Biology:** The air is breathable but tastes like copper and old leaves. Spores drift constantly. Extended exposure (4+ hours) requires a CON save (DC 12) or gain one level of exhaustion.
- **Navigation:** Everything looks like Oakhaven but wrong. Distances feel different. It takes a successful DC 14 Survival or Investigation check to navigate reliably.
- **Vine Walls:** Impassable without 10 minutes of cutting (axes/knives) or a fire source.
- **The Rot Pulse:** Every hour in the Upside Down, roll a d6. On a 1, a Vine Creature or Shadow Swarm has detected the party (3 rounds until arrival).
- **Communication:** The lights-and-alphabet method (from Oakhaven, using a modified string of LEDs and a letter map) still works across the dimensional barrier.
