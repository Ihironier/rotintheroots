# Encounters & Session Structure: The Rot in the Roots

This is a modern-day (2026) small-town horror campaign set in Oakhaven, Ohio. Replace any fantasy-feudal framing (lords, castles, etc.) with contemporary equivalents (school staff, police, town officials, corporate security, and federal contractors).

---

## Assumptions
- System: D&D 5e (adapt as needed)
- Starting level: 3
- Target end level: ~10–11
- Party size: 3–5 PCs
- Primary loop: **investigation → escalating incursions → conspiracy pressure → descent into the Upside Down**

---

## Session-by-Session Breakdown (12-session core)

### Session 1: The Dog Came Back Alone
**Objective:** Hook the party with a personal disappearance and the first undeniable clue.

**Key Scenes**
- Homecoming week energy at **Oakhaven High**; pep rally, flyers, local hype.
- News/gossip montage: missing pets, “coyotes,” a dead patch of woods behind the school.
- **Inciting incident:** Marcus Chen goes missing on his evening dog-walk; the dog returns alone—shaking, smeared with **black goo**.

**Encounters**
- **Roleplay:** Marcus’s family, neighbors, diner staff, the overworked deputy who “can’t take a report.”
- **Investigation:** Track the dog’s route; check doorbell cams; examine goo (Arcana/Nature/Investigation).
- **Tension beat (no combat required):** A streetlight pops; an unseen thing skitters across a roofline.

**Cliffhanger**
- A distorted emergency transmission on Fyndor’s scanner: “...containment team to the *south lot*... protocol VOSS-7...”

---

### Session 2: Paper Trails and Dead Ends
**Objective:** Establish the cover-up and give the PCs their first low-stakes threat.

**Key Scenes**
- The police station refuses to file a missing person report (“he probably took off”).
- Library research with Margaret Thorne: clippings about 1980s weirdness and older “cycles.”
- A town council meeting where the mayor pushes “normalcy” hard.

**Encounter: Shadow Swarm (Intro Fight)**
- **Location:** alley behind the diner / drainage culvert / creek footbridge.
- **Enemies:** 1–2 Shadow Swarms (tune to party).
- **Goal:** Survive and realize the creatures dissolve into oily residue.

**Clue Drop**
- The goo reacts to magnets / heat / UV (pick 1–2 consistent properties).

---

### Session 3: The Woods Behind the Bleachers
**Objective:** First true incursion into the dead zone.

**Key Scenes**
- Sneaking out during a homecoming event or after-hours.
- The woods feel **too quiet**; phone signal dies; compass spins.

**Encounter: Corrupted Deer Pack**
- **Enemies:** 2–4 Corrupted Deer.
- **Terrain:** thorny undergrowth (difficult terrain), patches of goo (acid).

**Discovery**
- A ring of dead earth and a **faint wet “breathing” sound**: the portal area (do not force entry yet).

---

### Session 4: Nexus on the Map
**Objective:** Put a name and face on the conspiracy.

**Key Scenes**
- A black SUV tails the PCs.
- A friendly adult ally warns them: “Stop. They don’t miss.”
- First mention of **Nexus Research**—a “biotech/agriculture” company outside town.

**Encounter (Flexible): Corporate/Federal Contractors**
- **Options:** social standoff, chase, or brief combat.
- **Goal:** PCs learn they are being monitored and threatened.

---

### Session 5: Allies, Gear, and Bad Ideas
**Objective:** Give PCs breathing room to prepare, recruit, and choose an approach.

**Key Scenes**
- Jim Wheeler (retired cop) reveals partial 80s truth.
- Gear montage: bolt cutters, hunting rifles, a drone, salt, UV flashlights, first aid.

**Optional Encounter: “Clean-up” Crew**
- Contractors erase evidence at a site the PCs visited.
- PCs can steal documents / ID badges / a keycard.

---

### Session 6: The Old Mill (Entry Point)
**Objective:** A contained dungeon-like crawl in a modern setting.

**Key Scenes**
- The Old Mill creaks; black goo seeps from cracks.
- Strange organic growth in the basement.

**Encounters**
- Shadow Swarm(s) in the upper floor.
- Vine Creatures in the basement.
- Environmental hazard: unstable flooring, falling debris, acidic goo.

**Discovery**
- A hatch / service tunnel: evidence of recent professional excavation.

---

### Session 7: The Gate and the Guardian
**Objective:** Force a decision about crossing over.

**Key Scenes**
- The tunnel leads to a chamber where reality is “thin.”
- The air tastes like pennies; electronics glitch.

**Boss Encounter: Theselhydra (or Negotiation)**
- The Theselhydra is not a random monster; it’s a **guardian**.
- **Win conditions:** fight it off, bargain, distract, or outsmart it.

**Choice**
- Enter the Upside Down now, or retreat and risk the gate widening.

---

### Session 8: Upside Down — Arrival
**Objective:** Establish survival-horror tone and rules of the mirror world.

**Key Scenes**
- Ash-choked air; predatory flora; familiar landmarks rotting.

**Encounters**
- Environmental skill challenge: navigation + shelter + exposure.
- One skirmish (Vine Creatures or Shadow Swarm) to teach the new rules.

---

### Session 9: Upside Down — The Hunt
**Objective:** Track Marcus and learn what the creatures are protecting.

**Key Scenes**
- Echoes of voices on the wind.
- A “nest” of corrupted wildlife.

**Encounters**
- Multiple smaller fights or one larger set-piece.
- A moral scene: Marcus’s belongings / evidence of partial transformation.

---

### Session 10: Nexus Facility (Real World Counterpoint)
**Objective:** Hit the human antagonist while the supernatural pressure rises.

**Key Scenes**
- Break-in, hacking, or hostage negotiation.
- Learn Dr. Voss’s plan and see how deep the operation goes.

**Encounters**
- Security patrols, locks, cameras.
- Optional containment breach: something gets loose.

**Major Reveal**
- The gate is being **kept open on purpose**.

---

### Session 11: The Demogorgon and the Seal
**Objective:** Final gauntlet.

**Key Scenes**
- Back at the gate (either side), the widening has accelerated.

**Boss Encounter: Demogorgon (Survive, Don’t “Win”)**
- Treat this as a cinematic threat.
- The real objective is completing the seal ritual / overload / collapse.

---

### Session 12: Aftermath
**Objective:** Consequences and epilogue.

**Key Scenes**
- Who made it out? What did it cost?
- Government response: cleanup, cover story, or a larger exposure.
- Tease: a new dead zone begins miles away.

---

## Encounter Building Notes

### Modern “Loot”
Instead of gold/magic items, reward:
- Evidence (documents, recordings)
- Access (keycards, passwords)
- Gear (UV lights, respirators, weapons, medical supplies)
- Allies (an honest deputy, a journalist, a whistleblower scientist)

### Scaling
- If your party is weaker: reduce number of enemies, add escape routes, run bosses as “hazard + clock” instead of straight HP.
- If your party is stronger: add environmental pressure (goo patches, darkness, time limits) rather than just HP.

### Tone Tools
- Keep fights short; keep dread long.
- Make the cover-up a constant pressure: they’re watched, recorded, disbelieved.
