# Encounters & Session Structure: The Rot in the Roots

Modern-day horror campaign set in **Oakhaven, Ohio, 2026**. Stranger Things tone. 8 sessions. Level 1–5 milestone progression.

---

## Assumptions
- System: D&D 5e
- Starting level: **1**
- Core length: **8 sessions**
- Party size: 3–5 PCs
- Tone: Mystery → creeping horror → conspiracy thriller → survival horror
- Stat blocks: see [CREATURES.md](./CREATURES.md)
- Locations: see [LOCATIONS.md](./LOCATIONS.md)
- NPCs: see [CHARACTERS.md](./CHARACTERS.md)

---

## Stranger Things DNA (Use Every Session)

- **Small-town normal vs. wrongness:** pep rallies, diner gossip, football/homecoming posters… and then dead zones, missing pets, and "coyotes" that leave impossible tracks.
- **Kids-with-gear energy:** bikes, flashlights, walkies, a police scanner, a slingshot, improvised weapons, DIY armor.
- **Analog weird:** cassette tapes, radios detuning, CRT static, streetlights popping when the Rot is near.
- **Lights as communication:** a string of garage/Christmas lights (or flickering fluorescents) answers yes/no questions; later, an alphabet wall.
- **D&D-as-metaphor:** the group labels monsters like they're entries in a Monster Manual ("Demogorgon," "Theselhydra," "the Rot").
- **Shadowy agency / front company:** "Nexus Research" is the public face; the real team is containment and research.
- **Body-snatcher paranoia:** Coach Hendricks isn't acting right. Nobody can prove it yet.
- **Upside Down rules:** ash/spores, predatory vines, organic growth in buildings, electronics glitching, time feels wrong.

---

## The Theselhydra Arc (Sessions 2–8)

The Theselhydra is not a one-off boss. It is the campaign's second protagonist — a massive five-headed apex predator of the Upside Down that has been held in chemical/sonic compliance by Nexus Research as a "containment asset." As the Rot destabilizes and the Nexus regimen fails, the Theselhydra becomes itself: territorial, aware, and doing something no one expected.

| Session | Theselhydra Status | PC Interaction |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| 2 | Omen — tracks, scale, creek sounds | No direct contact |
| 3 | First contact — territorial warning | Non-combat; it retreats |
| 4 | Shadow presence in the mill | Glimpsed, not engaged |
| 5 | Revealed as Nexus "asset" (from files) | Revelation via documents |
| 6 | Breaks free — open confrontation | Fight, escape, or negotiate |
| 7 | Encountered in its Upside Down territory | Marcus is in its lair (alive) |
| 8 | Final choice: ally or obstacle | Party decision defines ending |

**Key Question:** The Theselhydra has been keeping Marcus Chen alive in its territory. Why? The answer the PCs settle on — territorial claim, instinctive protection, empathy, something else — shapes the finale tone.

## Session-by-Session Breakdown

---

### Session 1 — The Dog Came Back Alone (Level 1)
**Goal:** Hook the party with a personal disappearance and the first undeniable clue.

**Opening:** Homecoming week at Oakhaven High. Posters everywhere. Marcus Chen didn't show up to D&D club.

**Key Scenes**
- Marcus's dog scratches at a door at 2 AM — alone, shaking, smeared in **black goo** that bleaches fabric.
- Police file minimum paperwork: "probably ran off."
- The D&D club room: the PCs gather, compare stories, name what they're dealing with.

**Investigation Beats (pick 3–4)**
- Doorbell cam footage glitches at the exact moment Marcus vanished (hard cut — no data).
- Fyndor's scanner catches: *"...containment… south lot… VOSS-7…"*
- The goo reacts to UV light — slow crawling pattern, eats through aluminum foil.
- Marcus's Christmas lights in his bedroom flicker: yes/no pattern when asked "Is Marcus alive?"
- Marcus's D&D notes: a creature entry for the **Theselhydra** — "five-headed water guardian, not malevolent."

**Encounter (Low-lethality)**
- **Shadow Swarm (Small)** — treat as 1 swarm, half HP. The goal is fear, not a TPK. It retreats when attacked with bright light.

**Cliffhanger**
- Something tall and flower-faced watches from the tree line behind the south bleachers. It's gone when the flashlight hits it. It was definitely there.

**Stranger Things Beats**
- The lights-as-communication moment (Christmas lights / yes/no)
- Scanner chatter as proof (prop handout opportunity)
- The D&D club naming ritual: "We're calling it the Theselhydra. Marcus named it."

---

### Session 2 — Static on the Line (Level 1 → 2)
**Goal:** Establish the cover-up and give the PCs the Theselhydra as a real presence.

**Key Scenes**
- Library with Margaret Thorne: clippings of "animal attacks" going back to 1987, then stopping cold.
- D&D club debrief: the PCs formalize their investigation. Fyndor maps scanner frequencies.
- A Nexus Research SUV does a slow pass by the school. Plates come back to a shell company.

**Theselhydra Arc — Omen**
- At Millbrook Creek: a single scale on the bank (iridescent black, dinner-plate sized, warm to the touch).
- Scanner chatter from the south lot: a low vibration that makes Fyndor's equipment rattle.
- Celeste's dream comes back. She wakes with her window open and a smear of black goo on the sill.
- The creek makes a sound the PCs have never heard before — low, resonant, like breathing through stone.

**Encounter**
- **Corrupted Animal (dog-sized)** — behind Patty's Diner, in the drainage culvert. Tense fight; it dies leaking goo.

**Revelations**
- Nexus trucks have been seen near the Old Mill at night for months.
- Coach Hendricks's storage room smells wrong and has a new padlock.

**Stranger Things Beats**
- Library research (analog, physical files, not Google)
- The scale prop — tangible, physical evidence
- Radio distortion: every time the PCs approach the creek, their walkies fill with wet clicking

**Level Up:** End of Session 2 — milestone to Level 2.

---

### Session 3 — Behind the Bleachers (Level 2)
**Goal:** First incursion into the dead zone; non-combat Theselhydra encounter.

**Opening:** The dead zone south of the school. A perfect ring of dead grass, 15 feet across. Cold spot. Something has been here recently.

**Key Scenes**
- The ring of dead earth at the creek edge — compass spins inside it.
- A Nexus Research field team is in the woods (two contractors, monitoring equipment). The PCs can observe, evade, or confront.
- The south bleachers fluorescent light pops and falls when the PCs approach the dead zone.

**Set Piece: The Bike Chase**
- Something keeps pace with the PCs in the dark. Radios fill with wet clicking. Phones show "No Service."
- It's larger than it should be. It stops at the edge of the dead zone and turns back.
- *This is the Theselhydra — not chasing them, herding them away from something it's protecting.*

**Theselhydra Arc — First Contact**
- The PCs see it clearly: five heads, serpentine, massive, moving through the trees like water.
- It makes no attack. It holds, watches, then withdraws into the creek.
- If the PCs stay still and don't advance, it doesn't advance either. It's a test.
- **DM Note:** Play this as a "I'm deciding whether you're a threat" moment. It doesn't want to fight. It wants them to go away. For now.

**Encounters**
- **Vine Creatures (3x Twig Blight variant)** in the dead zone brush.
- Optional: Corrupted Deer at the tree line if the PCs go deeper than the vine zone.

**Discovery**
- Five-toed tracks, heavy, circle the gate site in a 200-yard radius.
- A sound like breathing through a wall — the gate pulsing.

**Stranger Things Beats**
- Bike chase (literally on bikes, flashlight beams, snapping branches)
- Phones dead, compass spinning
- The creature that *doesn't* attack — scarier than one that does

---

### Session 4 — The Old Mill (Level 2 → 3)
**Goal:** Dungeon-crawl in a modern skin; find proof of Nexus's operation.

**Opening:** Boarded up. New chain-link with mismatched links. Tool marks on the boards. Someone has been in and out recently.

**Exploration**
- Ground floor: dust, rusted machinery, grain conveyors. Fresh cable runs along the north wall.
- Basement trapdoor: poured concrete, 1990s construction. Biohazard stickers. Numbered tags. Monitoring screens (gate status, Rot radius).
- The caged area: bent bars, fresh scoring in the concrete. Something big was here and is no longer contained.

**Theselhydra Arc — Shadow in the Mill**
- In the basement's northeast corner: a massive nest-mound of compressed vine and black goo, warm to the touch.
- Claw marks on the walls, 10 feet up. Five-toed, deep.
- The monitoring screen has a log entry: *"Containment asset relocated to south zone. Feeding schedule maintained via auto-dispenser. Behavior remains compliant."*
- The PCs realize: Nexus has been keeping something. This is where it lived.

**Encounters**
- **Shadow Swarm (Full)** in the rafters — drops when they hit the basement.
- **2x Vine Creature (Vine Blight variant)** in the flooded northeast corner.
- Optional: one Corrupted Animal loose in the basement.

**Loot/Evidence (Modern)**
- **Nexus Research keycard** (low clearance)
- **Incident report fragment:** *"...expansion beyond 4.2m aperture threshold… VOSS-7 protocol suspended… containment asset behavior unstable…"*
- **Handwritten note:** *"It's not staying in the south quadrant. It moves toward human contact. Recommend re-sedation."*

**Stranger Things Beats**
- The dungeon-crawl-but-it's-a-mill aesthetic
- The biohazard-horror basement
- The cage with bent bars: what was in here?

**Level Up:** End of Session 4 — milestone to Level 3.

---

### Session 5 — Nexus Research (Level 3)
**Goal:** Conspiracy thriller; reveal the full scope of what Nexus has done.

**Approaches**
- **Sneak in (night):** Stealth skill challenge. Two guards, camera coverage, motion sensor in server room.
- **Social engineer (day):** Disguise, fast-talking, using the keycard. DC 14 Deception/Performance.
- **Contractor contact:** The contractor who drinks at The Watershed. Buy him a beer and ask the right questions.

**Key Areas (see [LOCATIONS.md](./LOCATIONS.md))**
- Main Lab: black goo samples, biological cultures, still growing.
- Dr. Voss's office: project timeline, Oakhaven map with gate + dead zone + Old Mill circled.
- Server Room: 1992 sealing procedure (classified "impractical"). This is what the PCs need for Session 8.

**Theselhydra Arc — Revealed as Asset**
- In Dr. Voss's files: the Theselhydra is designated **"Containment Asset HYDRA-1"** since 1993.
- Personnel file: feeding schedule, sedation compound ("Compound V-19, administered via creek water supply"), behavioral compliance ratings.
- Recent note in red ink: *"V-19 supply interrupted September 14th. Asset compliance degrading. Do not approach south zone without Tier 3 authorization."*
- The PCs now understand: Nexus has been drugging it into compliance. As the drug runs out, the Theselhydra is becoming itself again.

**Encounters**
- Skill challenge: cameras/locks/guards (3 stages, DC 12/14/16).
- Optional fight: **2–4 Nexus Contractors** if alarm is triggered.

**Big Reveal**
- The gate is being kept open **intentionally** — Voss believes the Upside Down has extractable resources.
- Marcus Chen is listed as *"Civilian Exposure Event 2026-10-07 — ACTIVE — Containment Status: MONITORING."* He's alive. They know. They're not doing anything about it.

**Stranger Things Beats**
- The conspiracy-thriller infiltration
- The revelation that Marcus is "MONITORING" — cold fury moment
- The government-front-company's paperwork makes it *worse*, not better

---

### Session 6 — The Gate Wakes (Level 3 → 4)
**Goal:** Escalation; the Theselhydra confrontation; the gate widens.

**Opening:** Streetlights strobe across town in the early morning hours. Animals panic. Power grid brownouts on the south grid. Three houses on Elm Street lose power.

**Key Scenes**
- Sheriff Blackwell at the perimeter looking scared.
- The Old Mill tunnel has flooded — the gate is actively expanding.
- Nexus Response Team has mobilized: three vehicles, six contractors. They're not winning.

**Theselhydra Arc — The Break**
- The Theselhydra has broken free from Nexus's regimen. It has been moving between gates in the creek bed.
- **First Major Confrontation:** The PCs encounter the Theselhydra at the creek, between them and the gate.
- **It is not attacking.** It is panicking — the Rot surge is destabilizing its territory. The Nexus contractors are shooting at it. It is lashing out.
- **Win conditions for this scene:**
  - **Escape:** retreat to the treeline. The Theselhydra doesn't follow.
  - **Drive it off:** deal 40 HP of damage. It withdraws, injured, into the creek.
  - **Communicate:** DC 15 Animal Handling or Insight to read its behavior — it is in distress, not hunting. Approaching calmly (no weapons drawn) reduces the DC to 12. If the PCs back down and stay still, it eventually turns and enters the creek. This earns **trust** for Session 7.
  - **Desperate bargain:** if Celeste calls out "Marcus Chen" near the creature, all five heads orient simultaneously. Charged pause. Roll Initiative — but the DM may hold this moment.

**The Gate**
- Now 8 feet across. Crossing is possible with preparation.

**Choice**
- Enter the Upside Down now, or lose the chance as Nexus seals the area (12-hour window).

**Stranger Things Beats**
- Town-wide power surge (everyone in Oakhaven sees this)
- The creature that fought back against the people trying to control it
- The naming moment: "This is it. The Theselhydra. Real."

**Level Up:** End of Session 6 — milestone to Level 4.

---

### Session 7 — The Upside Down (Level 4)
**Goal:** Survival horror, navigation, finding Marcus.

**Entering:** The gate smells like copper and autumn leaves and ozone. On the other side: silence, ash falling like snow, blue-green bioluminescent vine light.

**Upside Down Rules**
- Phones: dead. Compasses: spinning. Radios: wet static.
- Rot Pulse: every hour, roll d6. On 1, Vine Creatures or Shadow Swarm incoming (3 rounds).
- Extended exposure (4+ hours): CON save DC 12 or one level Exhaustion.
- Navigation: DC 14 Survival or Investigation per major route choice.

**Theselhydra Arc — The Lair**
- The Theselhydra's territory: 400-yard radius from the creek crossing. Vine Creatures absent here.
- If the PCs showed trust in Session 6: they can move through this territory without the Rot Pulse trigger.
- If the PCs fought/drove it off: normal movement, but a charged encounter possible near its nest.

**Navigation Encounters**
- Skill challenge (3 stages, DC 12/14/16): navigate from the gate to mirror-Birch Lane.
- **Vine Creatures (3x full variant) + Shadow Swarm** in the mirror-school hallways.
- **Black Goo Hazard** patches near the creek mirror-bank.

**Finding Marcus**
- His room in the mirror-Chen house: walls covered in marker notes. He's alive, thin, dirty, functional.
- He has mapped mirror-Oakhaven from his window. He knows where things are.
- His theory: *"The Theselhydra isn't trying to kill me. It's been keeping the vine things away from this block. Every time one gets close, it comes."*

**The Cassette**
- His tape player: cassette labeled **HOME (unfinished)**. He recorded messages to his parents, his friends, whoever might find it. Thirty minutes of a kid saying goodbye, just in case. *Handle with care.*

**Return Logistics**
- Marcus can walk but is Exhausted (2 levels). Gate re-entry requires combined STR check (DC 14, 2 characters helping).

**Stranger Things Beats**
- Upside Down sensory immersion
- Finding Marcus alive (the "Will Byers" moment — earned reward)
- The cassette
- The Theselhydra keeping the block safe (still not named as benevolent — let the players decide)

---

### Session 8 — Seal the Tear (Level 4 → 5)
**Goal:** Finale. Seal the gate. Survive the Demogorgon. Define what happens to the Theselhydra.

**Opening:** Rot surge critical. Gate at 10 feet across. Nexus stabilization failing. Dr. Voss watching her project collapse.

**The Sealing Procedure**
- Found in the Nexus server room (Session 5): a 1992 ritual procedure, classified "impractical" (requires components from both sides simultaneously).
- **Skill Challenge (5 stages, DC 12 each):**
  - Stage 1: Position resonance markers at four cardinal points (Investigation)
  - Stage 2: Tune markers using the lights-communication frequency (Arcana/Technology)
  - Stage 3: Maintain channel while the Rot pushes back (CON saves DC 14)
  - Stage 4: Collapse aperture from both sides (Marcus signals from Upside Down side — 30 seconds)
  - Stage 5: Final seal — one character makes DC 16 check. Failure: seal holds but character takes 3d6 psychic damage and gains one permanent scar.

**The Theselhydra — Final Choice**
- The Theselhydra is at the gate. Not attacking the PCs. Watching.
- **If rapport was built:** DC 13 Animal Handling or CHA check to direct it — ask it to hold the Demogorgon off while the seal completes.
- **If rapport was not built:** obstacle. Navigate around it, drive it off, or work faster.
- **The Sacrifice:** If the Theselhydra fights the Demogorgon as an ally, play this as epic and finite. One or both may not survive. *This should feel like something.*

**The Demogorgon**
- Arrives at the gate in Round 3 of the sealing ritual. Drawn by the Rot surge.
- **Not a winnable fight at this level.** Cinematic pressure — run, decide, sacrifice.
- If Theselhydra is an ally: 3 rounds of distraction. Then both may be lost when the gate seals.
- If Theselhydra is not an ally: +1 difficulty to all remaining sealing stages.

**Win Conditions**
- **Complete the ritual seal:** Gate closes. Rot energy dissipates. Dead zones clear over weeks.
- **Overload the Nexus stabilizer:** Faster, messier. 2 rounds inside the facility with contractors resisting.
- **Collapse the tunnel:** C4 from Nexus emergency cache (Session 5). Collapses the Old Mill. Seals the gate physically.

**Dr. Voss**
- Obstacle (tries to stop the seal — it destroys years of data).
- Ally (if shown evidence the Rot is not a resource, she activates Nexus emergency protocol herself).
- Or she simply steps aside.

**Epilogue Beats (use one or more)**
- Federal "natural gas leak" cover story. Not everyone buys it.
- Marcus is home. He doesn't explain. He doesn't try.
- The Christmas lights spell: *THANK YOU.*
- One final flicker two weeks later: *SOON.*
- If the Theselhydra died: a single scale washes up on the creek bank. Celeste keeps it.
- If the Theselhydra survived and the gate is sealed: it is alone in the Upside Down. The gate is gone. *What does that mean?*

**Stranger Things Beats**
- The ritual-under-pressure finale
- The Demogorgon as cinematic spectacle (run, not fight)
- The Theselhydra sacrifice as the campaign's emotional peak
- The lights spelling words one last time

**Level Up:** End of Session 8 — milestone to Level 5.
