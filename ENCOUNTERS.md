# Encounters & Session Structure: The Rot in the Roots

This is a modern-day (2026) small-town horror campaign set in Oakhaven, Ohio, with a strong Stranger Things–style vibe (without using canon characters/locations).

---

## Assumptions
- System: D&D 5e
- Starting level: **1**
- Core length: **8 sessions**
- Party size: 3–5 PCs
- Tone: Mystery → creeping horror → conspiracy thriller → survival horror

---

## Stranger Things DNA (Use Every Session)
- **Small-town normal vs. wrongness:** pep rallies, diner gossip, football/homecoming posters… and then dead zones, missing pets, and “coyotes” that leave impossible tracks.
- **Kids-with-gear energy:** bikes, flashlights, walkies, a police scanner, a slingshot, improvised weapons, DIY armor.
- **Analog weird:** cassette tapes, radios detuning, CRT static, streetlights popping when the Rot is near.
- **Lights as communication:** a string of garage/Christmas lights (or flickering fluorescents) answers yes/no questions; later, an alphabet wall.
- **D&D-as-metaphor:** the group labels monsters like they’re entries in a Monster Manual (“Demogorgon,” “Theselhydra,” “the Rot”).
- **Shadowy agency / front company:** “Nexus Research” is the public face; the real team is containment and research.
- **Body-snatcher paranoia:** a trusted adult becomes “off.”
- **Upside Down rules:** ash/spores, predatory vines, organic growth in buildings, electronics glitching, time feels wrong.

---

## Session-by-Session Breakdown (8 sessions)

### Session 1 — The Dog Came Back Alone (Level 1)
**Goal:** Hook the party with a personal disappearance and the first undeniable clue.

**Key Scenes**
- Homecoming week energy at Oakhaven High (posters, practice, town pride).
- Marcus Chen goes missing on his evening dog-walk; the dog returns alone, shaking, smeared in **black goo**.
- Police refuse to file a real report (“probably ran off”).

**Investigation Beats (pick 3–4)**
- Doorbell cam footage glitches right at the moment Marcus vanishes.
- Fyndor’s scanner catches a clipped phrase: “...containment… south lot… VOSS-7…”
- The goo reacts to **UV light** (faint crawling pattern) and eats through cheap metal.
- A string of garage lights flickers in a **yes/no** pattern when asked “Is Marcus alive?”

**Encounter (Low-lethality)**
- **Shadow Swarm (small):** use 4–8 “swarm motes” (treat as 1 Swarm stat block but halve HP/damage). The point is fear, not a TPK.

**Cliffhanger**
- Something tall and flower-faced watches from the tree line behind the school stadium.

---

### Session 2 — Static on the Line (Level 1 → 2)
**Goal:** Establish the cover-up and give the PCs a trail.

**Key Scenes**
- Library scene with Margaret Thorne: clippings of prior “animal attacks” that stop abruptly.
- D&D club after school: the PCs compare notes and name what they saw (“Demogorgon” becomes shorthand).

**Encounter**
- **Corrupted Animal (single)** (a raccoon/dog sized thing): a tense fight in a drainage culvert or behind the diner.

**Revelations**
- Nexus Research trucks were seen near the woods at night.
- A teacher/coach acts strangely protective of the “renovated” basement area.

---

### Session 3 — Behind the Bleachers (Level 2)
**Goal:** First incursion into the dead zone.

**Set Piece**
- **Bike chase** with flashlight beams: something keeps pace in the dark; radios fill with wet clicking.

**Encounter**
- **Vine Creepers** (use 2–3 Twig Blights reskinned as vine-things; add 1 acid-sap hazard patch).

**Discovery**
- Ring of dead earth; a cold spot; a sound like breathing through a wall.
- A locker-room fluorescent light pops and falls when the PCs get close to the woods.

---

### Session 4 — The Old Mill (Level 2 → 3)
**Goal:** A dungeon-crawl in a modern skin; uncover proof.

**Key Scenes**
- The Old Mill is boarded up, but has recent tool marks.
- Below: fresh cable runs, numbered tags, and biohazard stickers.

**Encounters**
- Shadow Swarm (full) in the rafters.
- 2 Vine Creatures (use Vine Blight-lite, or reskinned Zombie + grasping vines).

**Loot/Rewards (modern)**
- A keycard with a Nexus logo.
- A shredded incident report referencing “Gate Stability.”

---

### Session 5 — Nexus Research (Level 3)
**Goal:** Conspiracy thriller session.

**Approaches (choose one)**
- Sneak in (night), social engineer (day), or steal access from an employee.

**Encounters**
- Skill challenge: cameras/locks/guards.
- Optional fight: 2–4 security contractors (bandit stat block reskinned with tasers).

**Big Reveal**
- The gate is being kept open on purpose.
- Dr. Helena Voss is the name on the project.

---

### Session 6 — The Gate Wakes (Level 3 → 4)
**Goal:** The supernatural pushes back; the guardian appears.

**Key Scenes**
- Streetlights strobe across town; animals panic.
- The PCs return to the woods/mill tunnels and find the tear widening.

**Boss/Set Piece**
- **The Theselhydra** (run as a terrifying obstacle, not necessarily a slugfest):
  - Win by **escaping**, **bargaining**, or **driving it off**.
  - If combat: use a weakened version (2–3 heads active at once; reduced HP).

**Choice**
- Enter the Upside Down now, or lose the chance.

---

### Session 7 — The Upside Down (Level 4)
**Goal:** Survival horror + rescue.

**Rules**
- Phones die, compasses spin, ash falls like snow.

**Encounters**
- Environmental skill challenge: navigate, avoid goo, find shelter.
- One hard fight: vine-things + shadow swarm.

**Discovery**
- Marcus’s trail leads to a “nest” zone; you find his belongings and a message: a cassette labeled **HOME**.

---

### Session 8 — Seal the Tear (Level 4 → 5)
**Goal:** Finale: seal the gate under pressure.

**Set Pieces**
- Demogorgon presence (treat as cinematic apex predator; direct fight = near-certain death).
- The real objective is completing the seal procedure (ritual/overload/collapse).

**Win Conditions (pick one as your canon)**
- **Overload the Nexus stabilizer** (countdown skill challenge while fleeing).
- **Collapse the tunnel** (explosives / engineered supports).
- **Ritual seal** using the lights/voices as guidance (costly, requires sacrifice or corruption risk).

**Epilogue**
- Oakhaven gets a cover story.
- One final flicker: a porch light spells a single word: “SOON.”

---

## Leveling Guidance
- Session 1: start level 1
- End Session 2: level 2
- End Session 4: level 3
- End Session 6: level 4
- End Session 8: level 5

## Reskin Quick List (Modern)
- Bandit → security contractor / desperate local
- Wolves → corrupted animals
- Haunted ruins → the Old Mill / tunnels
- Cultists → Nexus staff / compromised townsfolk
- Siege → town lockdown / roadblocks / curfew
- Underworld → Upside Down
